The United States’ Constitution in all its “wisdom” and really tiny handwriting, is a bastion of unclear words, missing commas, and generalized powers provided to persnickety agencies.  It is a plurality of vagueness that provides millions of people anxiety and terror on a yearly basis as we wait for the Supreme Court to learn how to use Twitter.

Now, the most general (perhaps Supreme Commander) of those clauses is the Postal Powers Clause - Article 1, Section 8, Clause 7 - which vaguely states that Congress (i.e. the Postal Service) can create postal roads and post offices.  It doesn’t delineate a mission that every US Citizen must be able to get mail, nor does it guarantee timely delivery on Christmas Eve (that’s why George Washington chopped down a cherry tree and realized he needed to invent Amazon.com).

But in the years since Thomas Jefferson scribbled that Clause with Benjamin Franklin cooing French literary discourse into his ear, the Clause found that once it hit puberty, it was a powerful Mutant with extraordinary powers! Not only to become an “independent” agency, grant constitutional rights of mail delivery to every citizen regardless of where they live in Alaska, suspiciously lose packages containing Marijauna, but also to revoke Zip Codes - strangling ordinary citizens from their Constitutional Right to have their aforementioned Postal Service!!! (but really what happened to all that weed)

I know - I can feel your heart racing.  It’s going a mile-a-minute, concerned that these Clause-havers will remove YOUR rights too!  Don’t worry. I’m here.  I am with you. Figuratively (and literally, don’t look behind you).

And so we must turn to Pennsylvania.  Rolling Appalachians, beautiful vistas, fresh Apple Cider! Can you hear the Amish?  So Lovely.  They’re doing Amish things!  With the butter and the Weird Al.  Paradise.  

When William Penn founded his Quaker homeload of equality and friendship, little did he know that years later, all those dreams would end up revoking Postal Services to one of his dear, quaint, small towns: Centralia, Pennsylvania.

And that’s not very friendly, now is it?

Centralia, Pennsylvania is a town of only 5 residents.  Constitutional rights aren’t just granted to towns of 6 people or more, so we must dig further.

In 1962, the same year as Dee Dee Sharp’s hit single “Mashed Potato Time,” theories (read as: conspiracy theorists/the ghost of Rush Limbaugh) suggest that Centralia’s Fire Department lost control over a trash blaze that inadvertently entered the Centralia Mine Shafts, creating an ongoing fiery death situation that some estimate will continue for the next 250 years.  This fire has spread to nearly 4,000 acres - all underground.  Can you imagine living above an ongoing fire? A wide scale evacuation was initially avoided due to incompetence and people not believing they were living over a hellmouth.  The Federal Government threw a few pennies into the fire pit, as did the State of Pennsyvlania, trying their best to get people to just get the fuck out of there.

Some did not, and local census reports there are still 5 US Citizens (and unknown number of fire monkeys) living in this area without mail service.

And so we must ask - if there are US Citizens, living in the United States, should they not have access to the same constitutionally guaranteed rights as those in even more horrifically remote places like Minneapolis?

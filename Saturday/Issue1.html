Issue 1: Postal Purview Permits Perilous Persecution - or does it?

Good evening Ken Dolls and Barbie Action Figures!  Welcome to the first (probably annual) issue of Saturday Suppertime Salon.  Let us bitch and moan like the best salon curlers and coffeehouse stick swirlers, and dig deep  - we must - for answers.  Or general complaints, that can work too.  Please address those to: PO BOX, GET THE FUCK OUT OF MY WAY, SHUT THE FUCK UP TEXAS (no comma) because as they say on Saturday Night Live: LIVE FROM MY HOUSE, IT’S SATURDAY NIGHHHTTT!!!!  *cue music*  *dips toe in Vodka*  Mmmm. Spicy.

The United States’ Constitution in all its ever loving wisdom and really tiny handwriting, is a bastion of unclear words, missing commas, and generalized powers provided to persnickety agencies.  It is a plurality of vagueness, that provides millions of people anxiety and terror on a yearly basis as we wait for the Supreme Court to learn how to use Twitter.

Now, the most general (perhaps Supreme Commander) of those clauses is the Postal Powers Clause - Article 1, Section 8, Clause 7 - which vaguely states that Congress/Postal Service can create postal roads and post offices.  It doesn’t delineate a mission that every US Citizen must be able to get mail, nor does it guarantee timely delivery on Christmas Eve (that’s why George Washington chopped down a cherry tree and realized he needed to invent Amazon.com).

But in the years since Thomas Jefferson scribbled that Clause with Benjamin Franklin cooing French literary discourse into his ear, the Clause found that it had the Mutant X gene buried somewhere inside of it, and now it has the power to revoke Zip Codes - strangling ordinary citizens from their Constitutional Right to have Postal Service!!!

I know - I can feel your heart racing.  Don’t worry. I’m here.  I am with you. Figuratively (and literally, don’t look behind you).

We must discuss the curious case of Centralia, Pennsylvania.  When William Penn founded his Quaker homeload of equality and friendship, little did he know that years later, all those dreams would end up revoking Postal Services to this small town.

And that’s not very friendly, now is it?
